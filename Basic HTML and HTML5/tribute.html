About

Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee, also known as TimBL, is an English engineer and computer scientist, best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. He is currently a professor of computer science at the University of Oxford and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

Tim Berners-Lee

Time line:

  • June 8, Born in London. His parents are Mary Woods and Conway Berners-lee.

  • He received a first-class bachelor of arts degree in physics at The Queen's College, Oxford.

  • Tim worked at D.G. Nash Limited where he wrote typesetting software.

  • Tim got a contract job as a software consultant in a company called CERN. He created the idea of sharing ideas quickly by a system called Enquire (this is similar to the world wide web).

  • Started in a new job called John Pooles image computer systems. This gave him experience in computer networking.

  • He went back to work at CERN with a permanent position.

  • With the help of Robert Caillilau Tim Berners-Lee produced the first version of The World Wide Web.

  • First website was built at CERN and put online on the 6th August. The World Wide Web was first put online in 1991, and "info.cern.ch" was the first World Wide Web server ever.

  • In 1992 there were over 50 web servers in the world.

  • Berners-Lee founded W3C (World Wide Web consortium) at the Laboratory of Computer Science (LCS) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Boston.

  • He became one of 6th members of the World Wide Web. They decided to improve the standards and allow anyone to be able to access freely.

  • He became a professor in the Computer Science Department at the University of Southampton, UK, in December 2004. There he worked on the Semantic Web.

  • Tim launched a charity called the World Wide Web Foundation in Uganda in 2009. This foundation goes towards the improvement and development of the world wide web.

    In 2009, he worked in a project set up by Gordon Brown to help make UK data more publicly available.

  • As of May 2012, Berners-Lee is president of the Open Data Institute, which he co-founded with Nigel Shadbolt in 2012.

  • The Alliance for Affordable Internet (A4AI) was launched in October 2013 and Berners-Lee is leading the coalition of public and private organisations that includes Google, Facebook, Intel, and Microsoft.

  • On 30 September 2018, Berners-Lee announced a new application made by open-source startup Inrupt based on the Solid standards, which aims to give users more control over their personal data and lets users choose where the data goes, who's allowed to see certain elements and which apps are allowed to see that data.
